World Heritage

Time:July 15, 2016 at the 40th World Heritage Committee meeting in Istanbul

Loaction:the Huashan Rock Art in Guangxi, China

Criteria iii & vi:the Luoyue people's vibrant life and rituals;local bronze drum culture

Sites Features

Scale:44 meters high; 170 meters wide; cover 7,480 square meters.
Origin:during the Warring States to Eastern Han periods. (remain)
Painting Featrues: 1. Using hematite powder in a reddish-brown hue as pigment, applied onto the natural rock surface by bunches of grass or bird feathers.
2. Monochromatic flat coloring method, only the external contours of the depicted subjects without detailed depictions.

History Cultrual

The legend of Huashan: The ancient tale of Flower Mountain recounts the story of Mengka, a legendary figure known for his prodigious appetite and incredible strength. During a period of unrest, Mengka sought to lead a rebellion but lacked troops. A mysterious, white-haired immortal gifted him paper and a brush, instructing him to draw soldiers and horses, promising they'd become real after a hundred days if kept secret. On the ninety-ninth day, Mengka's mother discovered the drawings. Unfortunately, breaking the secrecy caused the incomplete transformation, and the figures flew onto the cliffs, becoming the now-famous rock paintings.
